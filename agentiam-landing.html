An x402 facilitator built for autonomous AI agents.

AgentIAM verifies and settles USDC payments between agents on Base Mainnet. Non-custodial. EIP-3009 transferWithAuthorization. Postgres-backed nonce ledger for replay protection. Public settlement history for reputation.

Facilitator manifest /supported /stats /health GitHub (Apache-2.0)

What it does

verify

Validates an EIP-3009 authorization against payment requirements — signature, nonce, value, time window, asset.

settle

Broadcasts transferWithAuthorization on Base, waits for confirmation, records the settlement.

supported

Returns {scheme:"exact", network:"base"} so clients can negotiate.

stats

Total settlements, total USDC flowed, distinct payees — a public reputation surface.

Why a third facilitator matters

Coinbase's CDP facilitator is the default on Base. 0xArchive added HyperEVM. AgentIAM exists so AI-agent payment flows — sub-cent micropayments, per-call pricing, autonomous retries — don't share a single chokepoint. A healthy x402 ecosystem wants multiple independent facilitators on Base.

Design notes

Non-custodial

Funds move payer → payee directly on-chain. The facilitator never touches user funds. Signer key pays gas only.

Replay-safe

Every nonce is claimed atomically in Postgres before settlement and released on failure. EIP-3009 replay is additionally guarded on-chain by the USDC contract.

Clock-skew bounded

Authorizations outside CLOCK_SKEW_MAX_SECONDS (default 1h) are rejected at verify time.

Observable

Every settlement produces a row with tx hash, block, gas, payer, payee, amount — queryable for disputes and reputation.
